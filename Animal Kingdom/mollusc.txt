
Features of Molluscs

- Their bodies are soft and shelled. Under their shells are body covers, consisting of a thin layer of tissue called mantle.

- Other molluscs, other than black saliva, live in water and gills breathing.

- Open circulation is seen. Closed circulation is observed in cephalopods (cuttlefish, octopus), a class of molluscs.
